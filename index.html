Hey, I am Alen

I'm not the kind of designer who looks at something and decides what to do with it.

I pick it up. Turn it around. Look at it upside down. Watch other people look at it. Try to break it. See how the pieces fall. Then rebuild it.
That's how I design.

I'm an inter-disciplinary designer who thinks brands and products should actually talk to each other.

I didn't choose design. Design chose me, somewhere between a mechanical engineering textbook and a movie I couldn't stop thinking about. Over the years, I've worked across brand, product, strategy, and systems. Some projects changed businesses. Some changed the way I think. A few changed both.

Life Outside Design

Outside the world of brands, pixels, and product flows, I'm just someone trying to make sense of life with a mix of curiosity and humor. I love movies, not in a film-buff "I know every director" way, but in a "this one frame just changed my entire worldview" way. I'm often exploring new places, hanging out with friends, or getting lost in conversations that jump from philosophy to food to why the world works the way it does. I read, I observe, I overthink. And I genuinely enjoy the small moments - a good coffee, a long walk, a late-night giggle fit that makes everything feel lighter. Life outside design keeps me grounded, curious, and human... which is probably why I'm good at the design part.
